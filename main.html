Call Changes Practice

Set up for tower for call changes.

This application is designed to help notice ringers get used to call changes. It is based off a similiar application by Alison Stevens.

Call changes are a type of ringing where a conductor calls out which bells should swap places. A conductor will generally keep track of the whole row, but as an individual ringer you don't have to do that.

You do need to know:

  1. your own bell number
  2. the bell you are currently following
  3. ideally the bell two places ahead of you, if there is one
It can also be helpful to keep track of what place you're in.



There are two different styles of calling, you should find out which is used in your tower.

Calling up

Starting from rounds, if you're the 3, here are the three calls that affect you.

1. "3 to 4": you move UP (later) one place and follow the 4. The 4 will now follow the bell you *were* following, the 2, which is good to remember if you can.

2. "2 to 3": you move DOWN (earlier) one place and follow the bell that the 2 was following, the 1. This is why it's useful to know which bell is two places ahead!

3. "1 to 2": you stay in the same place but 1 and 2 swap under you. You were following the 2, but the 1 was just called to follow the 2 so now you follow the 1.

If a call doesn't include your number OR the number of the bell you're currently following, it doesn't affect you.

Calling down

Starting from rounds, if you're the 3, here are the three calls that affect you.

1. "3 to 1": you move DOWN (earlier) one place to follow the 1.

2. "4 to 2": you move UP (later) one place to follow the 4, the bell that's been called to follow the bell you *were* following.

3. "2 to lead": you stay in place but the 1 and 2 swap below you. You now follow the 1 which was two places ahead. This is why it's useful to know which bell that was, though the most important thing is to keep ringing steadily in the same place!
